FUTURES MARKETS;
Cotton Prices Stage Comeback From Steep Drop Early in Day

April 5, 1995

Cotton prices surged yesterday as mills took advantage of seven- week lows earlier in the session to replenish their inventories.

On other commodity markets, gasoline and wheat prices gained. The Commodity Research Bureau index of 21 commodities rose 0.84 point, to 235.37.

Cotton is beginning to look attractive to mills again after buying slowed when prices soared to $1.096 a pound on March 10, the highest closing price for an active contract since the Cotton Exchange was founded in 1870.
